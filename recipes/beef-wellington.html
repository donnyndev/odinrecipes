Beef Wellington

Indulge in the culinary masterpiece of Beef Wellingtonâ€”a savory delight featuring tender beef fillet enveloped in a layer of rich mushroom duxelles, encased in golden puff pastry. Baked to perfection, this classic dish promises a symphony of flavors and textures, making it a show-stopping centerpiece for any special occasion.

Ingredients

  • 2.5 pounds of beef tenderloin
  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened, divided
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onoin, chopped
  • .5 cups sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 2 ounces liver pate
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (17.5 ounce) package frozen puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 large egg yolk, beaten
  • 1 (10.5 ounce) can beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons red wine

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit (220 degrees Celcius)
  2. Place beef tenderloin in a baking dish. Spread 2 tablespoons softened butter over beef
  3. Bake in preheated oven until browned, 10 to 15 minutes. Remove beef from the pan and reserve pan juices; allow beef to cool completely.
  4. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in a skillet over medium heat. Saute onion and mushrooms in butter for 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool.
  5. Mixe pate and remaining 2 tablespoons softened butter together in a bowl; season with salt and pepper. Spread pate mixture over beef. Top with onion and mushroom mixture.
  6. Roll out puff pastry dough to about 1/4-inch thickness and place beef in the center.
  7. Fold up and seal all the edges, making sure teh seams are not too thick/ Place beef in a 9x13-inch baking dish, cut a few slits in the top of the dough, and brush with egg yolk.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 10 minutes. Reduce heat to 425 degrees Fahrenheit (220 degrees Celcius) and continue baking until pastry is a rich, golden brown, 10 to 15 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read between 122 to 130 degrees Fahrenheight (50 to 54 degrees Celcius for medium rare. Set aside to rest.
  9. Meanwhile, place resrved pan juices in a small saucepan over high heat. Stir in beef broth and red wine; boil until slightly reduced, about 5 minutes. Strain and serve with beef.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy!
